    Histology Notes

    Peripheral Blood Smear – Key Features:

    • Erythrocytes (RBCs): Biconcave, anucleate discs; central pallor ~1/3 diameter; transport O₂/CO₂.
    • Platelets (Thrombocytes): Small, irregular cytoplasmic fragments; essential for clot formation.
    • Neutrophils: 10–12 μm; multilobed nucleus; first line of defense, phagocytosis of bacteria.
    • Lymphocytes: 7–10 μm; dark nucleus, thin cytoplasm; central to adaptive immunity.
    • Monocytes: 12–20 μm; kidney-shaped nucleus; precursors to macrophages.
